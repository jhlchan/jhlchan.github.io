General Resources

Another good one is Quanta, the online magazine. There are two books from the past articles from Quanta:
  1. Alice and Bob Meet the Wall of Fire: The Biggest Ideas in Science from Quanta.
  2. The Prime Number Conspiracy: The Biggest Ideas in Math from Quanta.
From the introduction of the both books: as it happens, this book picks up where Particle Fever leaves off in telling the story of the quest to understand everything. The stories presented in these two companion volumes reveal efforts over the past five years or so to untangle the mysteries of rhe universe.

Like the magazine, this book is for anyone who wants to understand how nature works. It's for curisoity seekers who want a front-row seat for breakthroughs.

The story of Quanta began in 2012, weeks after the Higgs announcement. The magazine I envisioned would apply the best editorial standards, but its coverage would differ ratically from existing news outlets. It wouldn't report on anything you might find useful: no health or medical news, no breathless coverage of science breakthroughs. I wanted a science magazine that hepls us achieve escape velocity beyond your own small worlds but is otherwise useless like the way the LHC is useless. This useless magazine became Quanta.
